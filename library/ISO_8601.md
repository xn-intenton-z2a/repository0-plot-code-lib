Purpose and overview
- ISO 8601 is an international standard for representing dates and times intended to remove ambiguity between local formats. Implementations should prefer ISO 8601 when exchanging timestamps.

Date formats (exact forms)
- Calendar date (extended): YYYY-MM-DD (example: 2023-03-20)
- Calendar date (basic): YYYYMMDD (example: 20230320)
- Ordinal date: YYYY-DDD where DDD is day of year (001-365/366)
- Week date: YYYY-Www-D where ww is ISO week number (01-53) and D is weekday number (1-7)

Time formats and fractions
- Basic time: hhmmss (e.g., 235959)
- Extended time: hh:mm:ss (e.g., 23:59:59)
- Fractional seconds: append decimal fraction to seconds, e.g., 23:59:59.123 or 23:59:59,123 (decimal separator depends on representation)

Combined date and time with timezone designators
- Combined form (extended): YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss[.sss][Z|±hh:mm]
- Z indicates UTC. Offsets allowed in forms ±HH:MM, ±HHMM, or ±HH.
- Examples: 2026-03-20T12:34:56Z, 2026-03-20T12:34:56+01:00, 20260320T123456Z

Basic vs extended representations and allowed separators
- Extended form uses hyphen and colon separators for readability. Basic form omits separators for compactness.
- For CSV and machine interchange, extended form with T and explicit timezone is recommended.

Usage guidance for CSV and JavaScript Date parsing
- JavaScript engines reliably parse extended ISO 8601 strings (YYYY-MM-DDTHH:mm:ss.sssZ) with Date.parse/new Date(string). Prefer explicit timezone (Z or ±HH:MM) to avoid local-time ambiguities.
- When ingesting CSV, normalise timestamps to extended ISO 8601 with timezone before plotting.

Supplementary details (practical rules)
- Always include timezone designator where the source time zone is known. If timestamps are local and zone is unknown, record and document assumed zone.
- Avoid locale-specific date formats like MM/DD/YYYY or DD/MM/YYYY; they are ambiguous and engine-dependent.

Reference details (specification excerpts)
- Calendar date: YYYY-MM-DD or YYYYMMDD
- Time: hh:mm:ss[.sss] with optional timezone Z or ±hh:mm
- Combined: YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss[.sss]Z or with ±hh:mm
- Week date and ordinal date syntaxes are part of the standard and may appear in legacy data; handle or normalise them at ingest if encountered.
